Grammar — Possessive Case & Contractions

Choose the correct form: possessive case or contraction of "is"

Select the correct form for each sentence. Pay attention to context to determine if it's a possessive form (showing ownership) or a contraction of "is".

Important: The form "'s" can mean two different things: 1) possessive (John's car = the car of John) or 2) contraction of "is" (John's here = John is here).

Possessive Forms

  • Singular nouns: add 's
    John's computer, the cat's tail
  • Plural nouns ending in s: add '
    my parents' house, the girls' room
  • Irregular plurals: add 's
    children's toys, women's rights
  • Names ending in s: usually add 's (or just ')
    James's book OR James' book

Contractions of "Is"

  • Noun/Name + 's = is
    John's here = John is here
  • Pronoun + 's = is
    He's happy = He is happy
  • Question word + 's = is
    What's that? = What is that?
  • There + 's = There is
    There's a problem = There is a problem
  • That + 's = That is
    That's mine = That is mine
